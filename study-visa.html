Eligibility Requirements

Academic Qualifications:

Typically requires a certain level of academic achievement (e.g., specific grades, degrees) for admission to foreign institutions.

English Language Proficiency:

Proof of English proficiency through tests like IELTS, TOEFL, PTE, or Duolingo, depending on the country and institution.

Financial Capability:

Evidence of sufficient funds to cover tuition fees, living expenses, and travel costs for the duration of your study.

Acceptance Letter:

A confirmed Letter of Acceptance (LOA) from a Designated Learning Institution (DLI) or accredited university/college.

Medical & Character Requirements:

May require medical examinations and police clearance certificates to demonstrate good health and character.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What are the common English proficiency tests accepted?

Commonly accepted tests include IELTS, TOEFL, PTE Academic, and Duolingo English Test. The specific test and score required vary by country and institution.

How much funds do I need to show for a study visa?

The amount varies significantly by country and the duration of your program. It typically includes tuition fees for the first year and living expenses. Our consultants will provide a precise estimate based on your chosen destination.

Can I work while studying abroad?

Many countries allow international students to work part-time during their studies and full-time during breaks. Regulations vary, and we will inform you about the specific rules for your chosen country.
