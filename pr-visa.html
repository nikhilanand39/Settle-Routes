Common Permanent Residence Pathways

Skilled Migration Programs:

For individuals with in-demand skills and experience, often assessed through point-based systems (e.g., Canada Express Entry, Australia Skilled Independent Visa).

Family Sponsorship:

Allows citizens or permanent residents to sponsor eligible family members (spouses, children, parents) to join them.

Business & Investor Visas:

For entrepreneurs, investors, and self-employed individuals looking to establish or invest in a business abroad.

Provincial/State Nomination Programs:

Specific programs offered by provinces or states to nominate individuals who meet their labor market needs (e.g., PNP in Canada).

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the average processing time for PR visas?

Processing times vary greatly depending on the country, the specific program, and the completeness of the application. It can range from a few months to over a year.

Do I need an English test for PR?

For most skilled migration and some other PR programs, demonstrating English language proficiency through recognized tests like IELTS or CELPIP is a common requirement.

Can my family be included in my PR application?

Yes, in most PR programs, you can include your spouse or common-law partner and dependent children in your application. Specific criteria apply.
